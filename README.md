# RakshaKavach

“Raksha Kavach” is a Sanskrit/Hindi term where:

* **Raksha** = protection
* **Kavach** = shield or armor

So, **Raksha Kavach** means **“protective shield”** or **“spiritual protection armor.”**

It is commonly used in different contexts in India:

### Spiritual / Religious Meaning

In Hindu traditions, Raksha Kavach can refer to:

* A sacred thread, bracelet, locket, or talisman worn for protection
* Protective prayers or hymns like *Devi Kavach* or *Hanuman Kavach*
* A symbolic shield against negative energy, fear, or bad luck

Many people wear:

* Red/yellow sacred threads (*kalava*)
* Rudraksha bracelets
* Kavach lockets with mantras or yantras

### Modern Uses

The word “Kavach” is also used for protection systems in technology and defense, such as:

* Kavach — India’s railway anti-collision safety system
* Military protection systems and security programs

### Cultural Meaning

People often use the phrase:

> “Bhagwan ka raksha kavach”
> meaning “God’s protective shield.”
